Get paid to do research 🔬

You don’t wait until senior year. Here’s the actual ladder into a lab at UNR — several rungs come with a paycheck or a stipend.

PREP — Pack Research Experience Program

First-year and sophomore students get paid hourly to join a faculty research project. It’s designed to get you into a lab early. PREP →

NURA — Nevada Undergraduate Research Award

Up to $2,250 per cycle for research supplies, software, and travel. Open to all majors, no GPA cutoff — you just need a faculty mentor. NURA →

NSF REU — summer research (Biosensing)

A $6,000 stipend plus housing for a 10-week summer; chemistry students are explicitly eligible. REU →

Applying? A few quick tips

The process is simpler than it looks — and the most valuable move isn’t on the application at all.

1 · Apply to UNR

One application at unr.edu/apply — choose Chemistry as your major. Requirements and deadlines are in the freshman guide.

2 · Keep the core strong

Good grades in chemistry, biology, and math set you up for the major — and for the department’s scholarships.

3 · Email a lab early

The real edge: find a professor whose work excites you, ask about joining as a first- or second-year, and apply to PREP. Starting research early is what leads to everything else on this site.
